# A+ Research Brief - May 11, 2026

## Summary

This week's biggest signal is the **"Getting Down to Facts" report** (CalMatters, May 7) — a 1,000-page study by 100+ researchers calling on California to centralize oversight and scale proven interventions like tutoring statewide. It lands alongside the ongoing **NWEA dashboard data** showing reading scores stuck at pandemic lows while math inches back, and **AB 2555** moving through the legislature to auto-reclassify English Learners who score 4 on the ELPAC. All three stories converge on A+'s core positioning: schools need proven, human-led intervention — and they need it now.

### Topic 3: AB 2555 Would Auto-Reclassify English Learners Who Score 4 on ELPAC — 18,500 Students Stuck Annually

- **Category:** A (funding/policy) + B (problem story)
- **Source(s):**
  - [Many California students still labeled English learners despite testing as fluent — EdSource (March 2026)](https://edsource.org/2026/california-english-learners-bill/753660)
  - [Thousands remain classified despite fluency — Local News Matters (March 2026)](https://localnewsmatters.org/2026/03/22/thousands-of-california-students-remain-classified-as-english-learners-despite-fluency/)
  - [Bill text — AB 2555](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2555)
  - [Creating Coherence: Lessons on EL Reclassification — Stanford/PACE (March 2026)](https://edpolicyinca.org/sites/default/files/2026-03/r_mercado-garcia_mar2026.pdf)
  - [LPI: Long-Term English Learners in California](https://learningpolicyinstitute.org/product/ca-long-term-english-learners-report)
- **Why this matters for A+:** This is A+'s single strongest proof point turned into state policy. AB 2555 exists because 18,500 students per year score the highest possible ELPAC score but remain trapped as English Learners due to a "basic skills" gate. Stanford researchers found 39% of students scoring 4 weren't reclassified the next year. A+ directly helps schools reclassify LTELs — this bill validates the urgency. It's especially relevant for Tier A partners with confirmed Title III funding (Ocean Grove, South Sutter, Visions, Heartland, PCA) and Tier C prospects with unspent Title III funds (Community Collaborative Charter — $7,384 expiring).
- **Suggested angle for company post:** "18,500 California students scored the highest possible English proficiency score — and still weren't reclassified. AB 2555 aims to fix the system. We aim to fix the outcomes that get students there."
- **Roman take suggestion:** "The reclassification system is broken — the state is finally admitting it. But legislation doesn't teach a student to read. Intervention does. That's the part we do."
- **Danielle take suggestion:** "I've sat across from special programs coordinators who know their students are fluent but can't check the reclassification box because of a basic-skills test gap. AB 2555 removes the bureaucratic barrier. Our ELA intervention removes the skills barrier."

  - [High-impact tutoring 20x more effective — J-PAL/AIBM](https://aibm.org/research/the-strong-positive-effects-of-high-dose-tutoring-for-boys-and-girls/)
- **Why this matters for A+:** K-12 edtech VC has collapsed from $16.7B (2021) to under $3B (2025) — an 82% drop. Investors now demand "efficacy reckoning" — proof of pedagogical impact before funding. Meanwhile, Harvard's Strategic Data Project released a high-dosage tutoring implementation toolkit (Feb 2026) confirming that human-led, consistent, small-group tutoring during the school day is the gold standard. A+ is positioned on the winning side of both trends: human tutors over AI tools, proven outcomes over venture-funded promises. Competitors relying on AI-tutoring or tech-first models are losing funding and credibility.
- **Suggested angle for company post:** "K-12 edtech funding has dropped 82% since 2021. Investors want proof that products actually help students learn. The companies surviving? The ones with real tutors, real data, and real outcomes."
- **Roman take suggestion:** "The edtech gold rush is over. Every app that promised to 'disrupt' intervention is now scrambling to prove it actually works. We never had to pivot to efficacy — we started there. Human tutors. Measurable outcomes. That's the model that survives."
- **Danielle take suggestion:** "Harvard's new implementation toolkit says what I've seen in practice: tutoring works when it's consistent, when the tutor knows the student, and when it happens during the school day. That's not an algorithm. That's a relationship."
