## Brand colors

### Primary
- **A+ Navy:** `#1A3A52`
- Use for: headlines, primary CTAs, hero sections, footer backgrounds, data visualization frames
- This is the lead color in B2B. Institutional, credible, serious.

### Secondary
- **A+ Orange:** `#EF5829`
- Use for: data highlights, key statistics, accent moments, single CTA buttons
- The orange is now the accent, used sparingly for emphasis. Maintains brand family connection without losing institutional weight.

### Accent
- **A+ Gold:** `#F4A261`
- Use for: outcome callouts ("81% growth"), pull quotes, evidence highlights, badge elements
- Use sparingly — when it appears, it should mean something specific (a result, a credential, an evidence marker)

### Neutrals
- **White:** `#FFFFFF` — primary background
- **Warm Off-White:** `#FAF7F2` — content cards, alternate backgrounds
- **Charcoal:** `#2E2E2E` — primary body text
- **Light Gray:** `#E8E8E8` — dividers, borders, table cells

## Typography (updated 2026-05-19)

### Headlines
- **Font:** Playfair Display (Google Font, free)
- **Weight:** Bold (700) for H1, Semibold (600) for H2/H3
- **Case:** Title Case minimum on every headline. Lowercase headings fail brand-check v1.2.
- **CMS font-family specification:** when shipping to HubSpot or any CMS, the heading rule MUST specify `font-family: 'Playfair Display', serif;` so the rendered post matches the brand system.
- **Size guide:**
  - H1: 32-40pt
  - H2: 24-28pt
  - H3: 18-22pt

### Body
- **Font:** DM Sans (Google Font, free)
- **Weight:** Regular (400), Medium (500) for inline emphasis
- **CMS font-family specification:** `font-family: 'DM Sans', sans-serif;`
- **Size guide:**
  - Body: 16pt
  - Small: 14pt
  - Caption: 12pt

### Data / Statistics
- **Font:** DM Sans Bold (700) OR Playfair Display Bold (700), depending on graphic treatment
- **Treatment:** Larger size (typically 32-48pt) for hero stats, with descriptor text below in DM Sans Regular

### Why two fonts?
Playfair Display (serif) for headings and DM Sans (sans-serif) for body creates the visual hierarchy that mobile-first reading needs. Headings must be VISUALLY DISTINCT from body. Same-font heading/body pairings fail. Inter is retired from the A+ system as of 2026-05-19.

## Color combinations (do this)

✅ **Navy headline on white background** — primary B2B expression
✅ **Navy headline on warm off-white background** — case studies, long-form content
✅ **White headline on navy background** — section dividers, hero blocks
✅ **Orange data point on white** — stat callouts ("81% growth")
✅ **Gold accent on navy background** — premium evidence highlights
✅ **Charcoal body text on white or warm off-white** — all body content

## Color combinations (never do this)

❌ Orange-dominant compositions (parents-only signal)
❌ Orange headlines on navy (vibrating)
❌ Gold body text (too low contrast)
❌ More than 3 brand colors in one composition

## Image style

### Use
- Charts, graphs, data visualizations using brand colors
- Photography of educational settings (classrooms, intervention sessions)
- Diverse student/teacher imagery reflecting actual partnership demographics
- Document mockups (case study covers, evidence sheets)
- Whiteboard-style infographics (Charlie Hills approach — pulled high engagement)

### Avoid
- Stock photos of overly happy white families (B2B audience knows generic stock)
- Generic "diverse classroom" stock photos that look staged
- Apple emojis, chalkboards, pencil cliches
- Decorative fonts or visual clutter
- Stock business handshake photos
- Anything that looks like SaaS marketing

## Voice cues for B2B content

When this skill is active, content should:
- Use educational jargon appropriately (LTEL, MTSS, Title I/III, ESSA, reclassification, Tier 2/3 intervention)
- Reference research and evidence (NSSA, NWEA, MAP Growth)
- Lead with data and outcomes (specific percentages, growth points, study sizes)
- Speak to school administrators as colleagues, not prospects
- Acknowledge system-level constraints schools operate within
- Position A+ as a partner, not a vendor

## Banned words and phrases

- "All students" (banned across both brand kits)
- AI-detection vocabulary: "leverage," "delve," "harness," "foster," "fundamentally," "streamline"
- "Game-changer" / "best-in-class" / "revolutionary"
- "Empower" / "unlock potential" (corporate fluff)
- Em dashes (per Sergey Bulaev's 2026 LinkedIn AI-detection rules)
- Rule-of-three lists (AI fingerprint)
- Profanity (any content surface, hard rule)

## Data presentation rules

### When citing outcomes
- Always include sample size ("21 students," "47 schools")
- Always include benchmark comparison ("3-6x national average")
- Always cite source (charter program, NWEA assessment, time period)
- Round responsibly — "81%" not "81.4%" unless precision matters

### Hero stat treatment
- Stat in A+ Orange or A+ Gold
- 32-48pt bold
- Descriptor below in charcoal regular
- Source citation in light gray, smaller size

## Why navy lead for B2B

Charter directors and special programs coordinators are bombarded with vendor pitches. Visual signals matter at the scroll-by level. Navy lead immediately positions A+ as institutional and serious — the same family of colors as universities, education research orgs, and government agencies. Orange as accent (rather than lead) prevents A+ from looking like a consumer tutoring brand to a B2B audience.
