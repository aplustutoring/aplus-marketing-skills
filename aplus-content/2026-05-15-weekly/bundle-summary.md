## What this week's anchor is arguing and who it is for

The blog argues that federal K-12 funding volatility is now a planning reality charter LEAs have to budget around, not a one-time disruption to wait out. With more than $2 billion in approved K-12 grants currently withheld by the White House Office of Management and Budget (English learner programs, after-school supports, teacher PD, student support grants), and only the formula grants (Title I, IDEA) plus state-funded layers (LCFF supplemental and concentration, state intervention blocks) safe to plan around, charter directors and special programs coordinators face a different procurement question for 2026-27 than they did last year. The piece tells them how to size their Tier 3 intervention commitment to the non-volatile funding floor, then treat categorical grants as additive capacity rather than as the foundation. The audience is the charter LEA decision-maker: executive directors, special programs coordinators, intervention coordinators, and the business-office leads who route categorical funds. The piece doubles as Danielle's sales asset (she can link it in pitch emails this spring) and as a permanent SEO asset for the "federal education funding charter schools" query cluster.
