The White House is withholding more than $2 billion in K-12 grants that Congress approved in February.

For California charter LEAs planning 2026-27 intervention budgets, that changes the math.

What's still flowing on schedule: Title I, Part A formula grants ($18.4 billion preserved in the Consolidated Appropriations Act), IDEA ($14.2 billion preserved), school meal reimbursements, and state-funded layers like LCFF supplemental and concentration grants.

What's caught in withholding: Title III English Learner and Immigrant subgrants, Title II teacher professional development, Title IV student support, and 21st Century Community Learning Centers. The dollars exist on paper. Congress appropriated them. The executive branch is delaying disbursement, and the legal proceedings around the withholding are unresolved as of mid-May.

The operational read for charter directors: an intervention vendor contract executed this spring that assumes a Title III award arriving in August carries risk that did not exist in 2023 or 2024.

The charters that protect their Tier 3 program best in 2026-27 will be the ones that size their vendor commitments to the formula-grant floor first. Categorical grants that arrive fund additional capacity. Categorical grants that are delayed do not collapse the program.
