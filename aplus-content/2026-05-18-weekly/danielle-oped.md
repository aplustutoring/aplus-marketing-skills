In my second year teaching, our school got identified for additional support and we had three months to write the intervention plan. I remember sitting in a roundtable with the principal, the resource teacher, two parents, and the district coach, trying to figure out what the program was actually going to look like. We spent more time on the document than on the kids.

That experience is what I think about when CSI and ATSI designation letters land.

The charter LEAs we work with that get their plans approved quickly all share the same pattern. The program already exists. The roundtable already met. The data is already in the file. School director, special programs coordinator, classroom teacher, parents, A+ tutor. Everyone on the same page about which students, which dose, which subject.

At iLEAD's Tier 3 program last year, 20 students went through combined Math and ELA cohorts at 17 hours each. 16 of them improved. That data was the plan.
