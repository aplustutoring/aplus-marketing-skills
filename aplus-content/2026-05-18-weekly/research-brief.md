### Topic 1 — Category A (Federal/state K-12 accountability) — RECOMMENDED ANCHOR

**Headline:** "The 2026 California School Dashboard is coming. Charter LEAs at risk of CSI or ATSI designation should be locking their intervention plans now."

- **Why-now hook:** CDE issued the 2025-26 ESSA Aligned Release Letter on May 5, 2026, confirming the 2026 Dashboard release timeline. Per CDE's CSI page, the 2026 Dashboard determines federal designations for the next three-year cycle (2026-2029). This is the third year of the current cycle. Schools designated CSI or ATSI must develop and implement an evidence-based intervention plan within months of designation. Charter LEAs face a distinct rule: eligibility is measured across TWO Dashboard years (2025 + 2024), meaning a charter that just barely cleared 2025 can still be designated based on the combined two-year pattern. The schools that come into the fall with a Tier 3 program already running are not scrambling to "develop a CSI plan" — they ARE the plan.
- **Roman angle:** Charter directors who wait for the Dashboard release to start planning are the ones still scrambling when designation letters land in November. The schools that lock their intervention layer in May are the ones with results to show by the time their authorizers review them. That's the operational definition of being ahead.
- **Danielle angle:** What a CSI intervention plan actually looks like in practice. The stakeholder roundtable. The evidence-based intervention selection. The CAASPP/ELPAC/NWEA MAP referral logic. The targeted student cohort. Partner schools are already running the protocol; the only new variable is whether you have it in place before the November notification.
- **Target keyword:** "California School Dashboard 2026 charter CSI intervention plan"
