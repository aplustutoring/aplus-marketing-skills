**Target word count:** 150-250

---

If your child attends a California charter school, there's something coming this fall that parents do not usually hear about until it hits the news.

Every year, California publishes a School Dashboard that grades every public school on reading, math, graduation rates, and attendance. When a school's scores fall into the red bands for too many years, the state designates the school for federal "support and improvement." The 2026 Dashboard arrives this fall, and any school designated this round operates under an improvement plan for the next three years.

What this means for your family: if your child's school is at risk, leadership is already thinking about the intervention plan they will need to submit. That plan determines what extra support your child gets for the next three years.

A few questions worth asking the school this spring:

What did our 2025 Dashboard reading look like? Are any indicators in the orange or red bands? What's the school's plan if we are designated this fall? What intervention services would my child be eligible for?

Schools with a clear answer tend to be the ones running real intervention programs already. Schools that hesitate are worth a follow-up.

A+ Tutoring partners with California charter schools to provide one-on-one and small-group tutoring that fits alongside whatever intervention plan a school has built. If your school works with A+, the special programs coordinator can tell you what's available.
