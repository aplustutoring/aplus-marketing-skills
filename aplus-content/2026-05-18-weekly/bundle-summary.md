## What this week's anchor is arguing and who it is for

The blog argues that the spring before a 2026 Dashboard release is the right time to lock a charter LEA's intervention layer, not the fall after designation letters land. With CDE's May 5 Aligned Release Letter confirming the timeline and the two-Dashboard-year rule exposing schools that just barely cleared 2025, charter executive directors and special programs coordinators have a planning window now that closes in late summer. The piece tells them how to read their 2025 Dashboard for exposure indicators, identify the Tier 2 / Tier 3 student cohort, and execute vendor procurement in June-July rather than scrambling in October. The piece doubles as Danielle's sales asset for the early-summer charter conference cycle and as a permanent SEO asset for the "California School Dashboard 2026 charter CSI intervention plan" query cluster.

## Three suggested next-week topic seeds

1. **The ESSA evidence hierarchy explained for charter operators.** This week's blog referenced ESSA Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 evidence categories briefly. A follow-up could walk through what each tier requires, which intervention models qualify at each level, and what documentation a charter LEA needs to submit with a CSI plan. Useful evergreen.

2. **The county office of education partnership for charter CSI plans.** Charter LEAs designated for CSI must collaborate with their county office's continuous improvement coach. Most charter directors have no working relationship with their county COE. A practical guide to that partnership would help.

3. **Reading your 2025 Dashboard for ATSI risk.** ATSI is the subgroup-driven designation that catches schools where one student group (English learners, students with disabilities, etc.) underperforms even when the school overall is doing fine. Most charters don't realize they're at ATSI risk until the letter arrives.
