## What this week's anchor is arguing and who it is for

The blog argues that universal K-2 dyslexia screening, now law in 18+ states with fall 2026 implementation deadlines, creates a compliance and educational liability for charter LEAs that have built the screen but not the intervention pathway behind it. Under IDEA, a school that identifies a student as at-risk for a specific learning disability and fails to provide appropriate intervention can face a child-find finding. The blog walks the procurement window (build the structured-literacy Tier 2/3 pathway between May 27 and August 8), specifies the research conditions (same tutor across the year, 18-24 hour dosage, MAP-aligned content), and gives a 5-step playbook for charter directors and special programs coordinators. Doubles as Danielle's sales asset through August and as a permanent SEO asset for the "dyslexia screening charter school 2026 implementation" cluster across multi-state queries.
